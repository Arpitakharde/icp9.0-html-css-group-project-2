Seasonal Planting Guides

Recommendations for what to plant in different seasons.

    

Spring

Spring is a great time to plant cool-season vegetables like lettuce, spinach, and peas. You can also start your summer crops like tomatoes and peppers indoors to give them a head start. Ensure the soil is well-drained and rich in organic matter.

   

Summer

During summer, focus on planting heat-tolerant crops such as beans, corn, and squash. Regular watering is crucial to keep plants healthy in the heat. Mulching can help retain moisture and control weeds.

   

Fall

In fall, you can plant hardy vegetables like carrots, radishes, and broccoli. This is also the time to prepare your garden for winter by adding compost to the soil and mulching to protect plant roots from frost.

    

Winter

Winter is ideal for planning your garden for the next year. You can start indoor herbs and greens under grow lights. Make sure to protect any existing plants from extreme cold with proper insulation and covers.
